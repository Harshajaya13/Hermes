# 🎨 The Hermes Design Philosophy

> *"The interface should disappear so the user's thinking becomes the focus."*

Hermes does not follow typical high-dopamine visual trends. It is designed to look like a premium notebook crafted by someone who deeply respects your thoughts. Like a Kindle or Apple Notes, Hermes does not scream; it whispers.

---

## 👁️ Visual & Spatial Philosophy (Chapter 1 & 2)

*   **OLED Black Default:** Relieves eye strain during late-night reflections and maintains a distraction-free background.
*   **Intentional Whitespace:** Empty space is an active design element, not leftover dead space. Accidental empty space feels unfinished; intentional whitespace guides the eye and lets the mind breathe.
*   **No Visual Clutter:** A peaceful environment for documenting personal growth, removing visual stress and complexity.

---

## 📏 The Spacing Laws (Chapter 7 & 8)

Premium interfaces are made through spacing, not gradients or decoration. We enforce these eight spacing laws:

| Spacing Law | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Law 1: Room to Breathe** | Every element must have comfortable padding. |
| **Law 2: Active Emptiness** | Empty space is an active design layout Choice, not leftover gap space. |
| **Law 3: Proximity Grouping** | Related elements stay visually close. Unrelated elements stay apart. |
| **Law 4: Balanced Density** | No screen should feel empty; no screen should feel crowded. |
| **Law 5: Linear Eyesight** | The layout must guide the user's eye naturally. The user should never wonder where to look. |
| **Law 6: Action Visibility** | The primary action must be immediately visible without hunting. |
| **Law 7: Importance Mapping** | Space allocation matches element priority (e.g. *Today's Question* is large; *Settings* are small). |
| **Law 8: Conceptual Weight** | Visual footprint matches functional weight (e.g., a *Math Block* is prominent; *Delete Account* is tiny). |

---

## 📇 Card & Component Rules (Chapter 4, 5, 9)

Cards must be lightweight and functional:
*   **No Screen Domination:** Cards are grouping boundaries, not decorative boxes.
*   **One Clear Purpose:** Each card carries a single functional goal.
*   **Compact Height:** Card heights must remain limited to maintain high vertical information density.
*   **Outlined Icons:** Muted, outlined icons are used for purpose, never pure decoration. Emojis are reserved for user-controlled naming only.

---

## 🔠 Typography Hierarchy (Chapter 3)

The typography structure remains strictly locked to prevent visual noise:

```text
[Screen Title] (Loudest emphasis)
      ↓
[Section Title] (Structural boundary)
      ↓
  [Block Title] (Context indicator)
      ↓
  [Item Title] (Standard emphasis)
      ↓
    [Body] (Whispering, readable book style)
      ↓
  [Metadata] (Muted, secondary info)
```

---

## 🎬 Motion & Transitions (Chapter 6)

Animations must represent structural changes, never just look "cool":
*   **Fades & Slides:** Guide screen context changes.
*   **Scale transitions:** Represent expansion/contraction of item elements.
*   **Tactile Feedback:** Muted haptic timing mirrors actual physical inputs.

---

## 🧠 Emotional Objective (Chapter 10)

Hermes is not designed to look "futuristic" or "AI-centric." It is designed to feel:
$$\text{Calm} \quad \bullet \quad \text{Quiet} \quad \bullet \quad \text{Personal} \quad \bullet \quad \text{Trustworthy} \quad \bullet \quad \text{Warm} \quad \bullet \quad \text{Timeless}$$

When the screen turns off at 10:45 PM after you record an Evolutio, the app should leave you with one clear feeling: **"Today mattered."**
