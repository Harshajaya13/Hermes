# Reflections

## Purpose

Reflection is where information becomes understanding.

Reading an article, solving a question, or recording an observation does not automatically change the way you think.

Learning only becomes meaningful when you pause, examine your experience, and ask yourself:

> "What did I actually understand?"

Reflections exist to preserve that moment.

Rather than storing information, they store the way your understanding changes over time.

---

## Why Reflections Exist

Modern software encourages speed.

Finish the task.

Read the article.

Watch the video.

Move on.

Hermes intentionally slows that process down.

Instead of immediately chasing the next piece of information, Reflection encourages you to stop for a moment and process what you have just experienced.

That pause is often where the deepest learning occurs.

---

## Creating a Reflection

A Reflection can be written at any time.

It may follow:

- Solving a Question
- Reading an Article
- Recording an Observation
- Developing an Idea
- Completing a Project
- Experiencing something meaningful in everyday life

Reflections are not limited to studying.

Anything capable of changing your perspective deserves reflection.

---

## What Should a Reflection Contain?

A Reflection is not a summary.

It is not a copy of your notes.

Instead, it answers questions like:

- What changed in my understanding?
- What surprised me?
- What mistake was I making?
- What will I do differently from now on?
- Why does this matter?

The focus is always on understanding rather than information.

---

## Connected Knowledge

Every Reflection can be connected with other Items inside Hermes.

Examples include:

- Questions
- Articles
- Notes
- Observations
- Ideas
- Evolutios

These connections preserve the journey that led to the Reflection.

Months later, you can retrace exactly how your thinking evolved.

---

## Editing Reflections

Understanding is never finished.

As your knowledge grows, you may revisit old Reflections and refine them.

Perhaps your explanation becomes clearer.

Perhaps you discover a better perspective.

Perhaps your original Reflection was incomplete.

Hermes encourages continuous refinement instead of treating writing as permanent.

Every Reflection remains editable.

Growth should never be locked.

---

## From Reflection to Evolutio

Most Reflections capture understanding.

Some capture transformation.

When a Reflection represents a genuine shift in your thinking, it can be promoted into an Evolutio.

Not every Reflection needs to become one.

Only those that permanently change the way you think deserve that distinction.

This keeps Evolutios meaningful instead of becoming another collection of ordinary notes.

---

## Looking Back

One of the most valuable experiences inside Hermes is revisiting old Reflections.

Reading how you once understood a concept often reveals how much you have grown.

Sometimes you discover mistakes you no longer make.

Sometimes you rediscover ideas worth exploring again.

Reflections create a timeline of your thinking rather than simply documenting your work.

---

## Long-Term Value

Over months and years, Reflections become conversations with your past self.

They reveal not only what you learned, but how your perspective changed.

This makes Hermes more than a knowledge manager.

It becomes a record of your intellectual growth.

---

## Why This Workflow?

Information is everywhere.

Understanding is personal.

Reflection exists because genuine learning happens not when information enters your mind, but when your mind changes because of it.

Hermes preserves those moments so they are never forgotten.
