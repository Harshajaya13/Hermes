
# Blocks

## Purpose

Blocks divide a large Domain into focused areas of study.

A Domain represents a broad, long-term discipline, while a Block represents a specific subject within that discipline.

For example:

Engineering

↓

Python

↓

Questions

Articles

Ideas

Notes

By introducing Blocks, Hermes keeps knowledge organized without forcing everything into one enormous collection.

---

## Why Blocks Exist

Learning rarely happens across an entire Domain at once.

You don't study all of Engineering in one session.

Instead, you focus on one subject at a time.

Python.

Probability.

Operating Systems.

Deep Learning.

Blocks exist to create these focused environments where every piece of related knowledge naturally belongs together.

Instead of asking:

> "Where should I save this?"

Hermes encourages:

> "Which subject does this belong to?"

This makes navigation simpler and keeps every Block meaningful even after years of learning.

---

## Entering a Block

Selecting a Block opens the Items inside it.

A Block becomes your working environment.

Here you'll find every type of knowledge related to that subject, including:

• Questions

• Articles

• Notes

• Ideas

• Observations

• Reflections

• Evolutios

Each Item contributes to your understanding from a different perspective.

---

## Pinning Blocks

Some Blocks become part of your everyday routine.

For example:

• Probability

• PyTorch

• Linux

• Startup

Rather than navigating through the hierarchy every day, frequently used Blocks can be pinned.

Pinned Blocks appear directly on the Home screen, allowing quick access while preserving the overall organization.

Pinning never duplicates or moves your knowledge.

It simply creates a shortcut.

---

## Block Settings

Each Block provides its own management options.

From here you can:

• Rename the Block

• Pin or unpin it

• Archive it when it is no longer active

• Manage future settings as Hermes evolves

Every Block remains independent.

Changes inside one Block never affect another.

---

## Long-Term Learning

Blocks are designed for continuous growth.

Unlike temporary folders that eventually become abandoned, a Block evolves alongside your understanding.

A Python Block may begin with basic syntax, gradually accumulate notes, articles, reflections, and questions, and eventually become a complete knowledge base built over months or even years.

Hermes treats Blocks as living collections rather than static folders.

---

## The Next Step

Once inside a Block, Hermes presents its Items.

Items are the smallest units of knowledge inside Hermes.

Each Item type serves a different purpose in helping you understand, reflect, and grow.
