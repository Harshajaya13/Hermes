# Articles

## Purpose

Articles are where Hermes slows everything down.

The modern internet is designed to maximize attention rather than understanding. Pop-ups, advertisements, notifications, autoplay videos, and endless recommendations constantly interrupt the reading experience.

Hermes removes that noise.

An Article exists to create a quiet environment where your attention belongs entirely to the knowledge in front of you.

Reading should feel calm, intentional, and uninterrupted.

---

## Why Articles Exist

Saving hundreds of bookmarks does not mean you will ever read them.

Reading an article once does not mean you understood it.

Hermes treats Articles as part of a long-term learning journey rather than temporary browser tabs.

Instead of asking:

> "How many articles have I collected?"

Hermes encourages a different question:

> "How many ideas have I truly understood?"

That small shift changes reading from collecting information into building understanding.

---

## Reading Inside Hermes

Articles are rendered locally inside Hermes.

Instead of opening the original webpage, Hermes presents the content in a distraction-free reader designed specifically for learning.

The reader supports:

- Clean typography
- Markdown rendering
- LaTeX mathematics
- Code blocks
- Comfortable spacing
- Offline reading

The goal is simple.

Once reading begins, the interface should disappear.

Only the knowledge should remain.

---

## Mark as Read

Completing an article does not remove it from your knowledge base.

Instead, Hermes records that you have read it.

The article remains available for future reference whenever you need it again.

Inside Block lists, previously read articles are displayed using a softer visual appearance, allowing you to immediately distinguish between:

- New articles
- Previously surfaced articles
- Articles you have already completed

This becomes especially valuable when your library grows into hundreds or even thousands of articles.

---

## Reflection

Reading is only the first step.

Sometimes an article simply provides information.

Other times, it changes how you think.

Whenever that happens, Hermes encourages you to write a Reflection.

A Reflection answers a simple question:

> "What changed in my understanding after reading this?"

Reflections are entirely optional.

Not every article deserves one.

But when an article genuinely changes your perspective, recording that realization allows your future self to revisit not only the article, but also the way it shaped your thinking.

---

## Articles in Today's Pursuit

Articles can appear automatically inside Today's Pursuit when they are imported through Manual Collections.

Rather than asking you to decide what to read every morning, Hermes gradually surfaces articles according to the Daily Limit configured for each collection.

For example:

Reading Collection

Daily Limit: 1

Today:

One carefully selected article.

Tomorrow:

Another article.

This keeps reading sustainable instead of overwhelming.

---

## Long-Term Reading

Hermes is designed for readers who continuously collect knowledge.

Instead of maintaining hundreds of forgotten browser bookmarks, Articles become part of an organized knowledge system.

You can always return to an article, reread it, update your Reflection, or connect it with other knowledge inside your workspace.

Nothing is lost.

Nothing becomes buried.

Everything remains discoverable.

---

## Why This Workflow?

Most reading applications measure completion.

Hermes measures understanding.

Finishing an article is useful.

Remembering how that article changed your thinking is far more valuable.

Articles are not simply things to finish.

They are opportunities to deepen your understanding, one thoughtful reading session at a time.
