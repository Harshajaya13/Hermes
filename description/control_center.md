# Control Center

## Purpose

The Control Center is where you manage Hermes itself.

While the rest of Hermes is dedicated to learning, the Control Center is dedicated to maintaining your knowledge system.

Everything that affects your workspace, collections, imports, exports, settings, and organization begins here.

Think of it as the operating room of your personal knowledge system.

---

## Why A Control Center Exists

As your knowledge grows, managing it becomes just as important as collecting it.

Over time you may create:

- Multiple Workspaces
- Hundreds of Blocks
- Thousands of Questions
- Thousands of Articles
- Numerous Imports

Rather than scattering management features throughout the application, Hermes brings them together into one dedicated location.

Everything administrative belongs here.

Everything related to learning remains outside.

This separation keeps the rest of Hermes clean and distraction free.

---

## Workspace Management

The Control Center allows you to manage every Workspace stored inside Hermes.

From here you can:

- Create new Workspaces
- Switch between existing Workspaces
- Remove unused Workspaces
- Lock private Workspaces
- Configure Workspace settings

Every Workspace remains completely independent.

Changing one Workspace never affects another.

---

## Knowledge Pipeline

The Control Center is also home to the Knowledge Pipeline.

From here you can:

- Create Manual Collections
- Import JSON collections
- Import single articles
- Configure Import Settings
- Read the Collection Guide

Everything related to bringing knowledge into Hermes begins here.

---

## Export & Ownership

Your knowledge belongs to you.

The Control Center provides quick access to exporting your Workspaces and Collections.

Whether you want to create a backup, migrate to another device, or share knowledge with someone else, everything starts here.

Ownership should always remain one click away.

---

## Archive

Not every piece of knowledge needs to remain active forever.

Archived Items remain safely preserved without cluttering your daily workspace.

Nothing is permanently lost.

Whenever you need something again, simply restore it from the Archive.

Hermes prefers preservation over deletion.

---

## Guides

Hermes intentionally includes built-in guides.

These explain:

- How Manual Collections work
- Import formats
- Knowledge Pipelines
- Daily Scheduling
- JSON structures

Rather than sending users to external documentation, Hermes teaches itself from within.

Learning the application should feel as natural as using it.

---

## Settings

Application-wide preferences are also managed from the Control Center.

Examples include:

- Appearance
- Reading preferences
- Workspace behavior
- Visibility options
- Future customization features

These settings affect Hermes itself rather than individual Items.

---

## Designed To Stay Out Of Your Way

Most days you won't spend much time inside the Control Center.

That is intentional.

Its purpose is to organize your system so that the rest of Hermes can remain calm, focused, and free from unnecessary complexity.

You configure.

Hermes quietly does the rest.

---

## Philosophy

The Control Center exists so that management never interrupts learning.

Administration should happen in one place.

Understanding should happen everywhere else.

Hermes separates these responsibilities intentionally, allowing your attention to remain focused on knowledge rather than configuration.

A well-organized system should feel almost invisible.

The Control Center exists to keep it that way.
