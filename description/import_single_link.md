# Import Single Link

## Purpose

Not every piece of knowledge arrives as a large collection.

Sometimes you discover a single article, research paper, blog post, or documentation page that deserves a place inside your knowledge system.

Import Single Link exists for those moments.

Instead of bookmarking the page and forgetting about it, Hermes allows you to immediately import it into your own workspace.

---

## Why Single Link Import Exists

Imagine you're reading online and suddenly find an excellent article.

Traditionally you would:

- Bookmark it.
- Leave the tab open.
- Save it to a reading list.

Weeks later, it is buried beneath hundreds of other bookmarks.

Hermes takes a different approach.

The moment you decide the article is valuable, you can import it directly into your knowledge system where it becomes searchable, schedulable, and permanently yours.

---

## How It Works

Simply paste the article URL.

Hermes will:

- Download the content.
- Extract the readable article.
- Remove unnecessary webpage clutter.
- Convert it into a local Hermes Article.
- Save it inside the selected Manual Collection.

From that moment onward, the article belongs to your workspace rather than the internet.

---

## Assigning the Article

Before importing, choose where the article should live.

Select:

- Domain
- Block
- Manual Collection

For example:

Engineering

↓

Operating Systems

↓

Linux Articles

The article immediately becomes part of that collection and follows the same scheduling rules as every other imported article.

---

## Automatic Scheduling

Importing a single article does not immediately place it into Today's Pursuit.

Instead, Hermes adds it to the selected collection.

The scheduler will surface it naturally according to the Daily Limit configured for that collection.

This keeps your daily reading intentional rather than overwhelming.

---

## Offline Ownership

Once imported, the article becomes part of your local workspace.

You can continue reading it even without an internet connection.

If the original website disappears in the future, your copy remains available.

Hermes believes that knowledge you intentionally saved should remain yours.

---

## Reader Experience

Imported articles are rendered using Hermes' distraction-free reader.

Advertisements, unnecessary layouts, and visual noise are removed.

The focus remains entirely on the content.

Reading should feel calm, comfortable, and uninterrupted.

---

## Growing Your Library

Over time, a single imported article becomes hundreds.

Instead of maintaining an ever-growing collection of forgotten browser bookmarks, you gradually build a carefully organized library that Hermes can surface exactly when you need it.

One meaningful article today is often more valuable than collecting fifty you never read.

---

## Philosophy

The internet is temporary.

Links break.

Websites change.

Services disappear.

Hermes allows you to preserve the knowledge that matters before it is lost.

A bookmark remembers where information was.

Hermes preserves the information itself.

That difference transforms a temporary webpage into a permanent part of your personal knowledge system.
