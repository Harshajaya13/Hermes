# Domains

## Purpose

Domains represent the highest level of organization inside Hermes.

A Domain is not a folder for random files. Instead, it represents a long-term area of your life that you continuously learn, build, or improve.

Examples include:

- Computer Science
- Mathematics
- Startup
- Philosophy
- Health
- Finance
- Personal Growth

Think of a Domain as a place where knowledge naturally belongs for years rather than days.

---

## Why Domains Exist

Without organization, every note eventually becomes difficult to find.

Hermes intentionally asks you to assign knowledge to a Domain before storing it. This small amount of friction keeps your knowledge base organized instead of becoming another collection of scattered notes.

Rather than asking:

> "Where did I save this?"

Hermes encourages you to think:

> "Which part of my life does this belong to?"

---

## Navigating Domains

Selecting a Domain opens its Blocks.

A Domain does not directly contain notes or questions.

Instead, it contains multiple Blocks that divide large subjects into smaller areas.

For example:

Computer Science

↓

Python

↓

Questions

↓

Articles

↓

Ideas

↓

Notes

This hierarchy keeps knowledge easy to navigate even after years of use.

---

## Pinning Domains

Frequently used Domains can be pinned.

Pinned Domains appear directly on the Home screen, allowing quick access without navigating through the entire hierarchy every day.

Pinning only changes visibility.

It does not move or duplicate your data.

---

## Domain Settings

Each Domain has its own options menu.

From here you can manage the Domain, rename it, adjust its appearance, or perform future management actions without affecting other Domains.

Domains are independent from one another.

Changing one Domain never changes another.

---

## Why This Design?

Many note-taking applications organize everything using folders.

Hermes instead organizes knowledge using Domains because learning is usually continuous rather than temporary.

Programming is not a folder.

Mathematics is not a folder.

Philosophy is not a folder.

They are lifelong disciplines.

Domains reflect that idea.

---

Once you choose a Domain, you enter its Blocks, where broad areas become focused subjects ready to hold your knowledge.
