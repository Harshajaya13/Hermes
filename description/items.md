# Items

## Purpose

Everything you store inside Hermes is an Item.

An Item is the smallest unit of knowledge within your workspace. Rather than forcing every piece of information into a single note format, Hermes recognizes that different kinds of thinking deserve different environments.

A question is not a note.

An observation is not an idea.

An article is not a reflection.

Each Item type exists for a different stage of learning.

---

## Why Different Item Types Exist

Most knowledge applications store everything as plain text.

Over time, every note begins to look the same.

Questions become mixed with ideas.

Research becomes mixed with observations.

Reflections become buried beneath copied articles.

Eventually, finding anything meaningful becomes difficult.

Hermes avoids this by giving every type of knowledge its own dedicated workflow.

Instead of asking:

> "Where should I write this?"

Hermes encourages:

> "What kind of knowledge is this?"

That simple decision keeps your workspace organized naturally.

---

## Item Types

Every Block can contain several different kinds of Items.

Each one serves a unique purpose.

### Questions

Questions encourage active learning.

Instead of passively consuming information, you attempt an answer, compare it with the official solution, reflect on your understanding, and gradually improve over time.

Questions are designed to strengthen understanding rather than memory.

---

### Articles

Articles provide a calm reading experience.

Hermes removes unnecessary distractions and allows you to focus entirely on the content.

After reading, you may simply mark the article as read or write a Reflection if the article changed your thinking.

Reading is not measured by completion.

It is measured by understanding.

---

### Notes

Notes capture knowledge in your own words.

Rather than copying information directly from an article, Notes represent your interpretation after understanding the topic.

They also support keywords and connections to related Items, allowing knowledge to naturally form a network instead of remaining isolated.

---

### Observations

Observations record small moments that are easy to forget.

A conversation.

A pattern.

A realization.

Something you noticed during everyday life.

Many observations seem insignificant when first written.

Months later, they often become the foundation of much larger ideas.

Observations can later be converted into Reflections or Ideas as they mature.

---

### Ideas

Ideas are possibilities.

Sometimes a single sentence grows into an entire project.

Hermes allows ideas to evolve naturally by expanding them over time, recording potential applications, connecting them with related knowledge, promoting them into Projects, or archiving them until the right moment arrives.

Ideas are never expected to be complete on the first day.

---

### Reflections

Reflection is where learning becomes understanding.

Reading something does not guarantee that it changed you.

Reflection asks one simple question:

> "What changed in the way you think?"

Reflections help transform information into personal understanding.

---

### Evolutios

An Evolutio represents a genuine cognitive shift.

Not every Reflection becomes an Evolutio.

Only those moments where your understanding fundamentally changes deserve this distinction.

Hermes considers Evolutios to be the true measure of growth.

---

## Navigation

Selecting any Item opens its dedicated workspace.

Each Item type provides tools specifically designed for that form of knowledge.

Questions focus on solving.

Articles focus on reading.

Ideas focus on expansion.

Observations focus on noticing.

Notes focus on understanding.

This allows every workflow to remain simple while still providing powerful features.

---

## Growing Your Knowledge

Over time, a single Block naturally becomes a rich collection of interconnected knowledge.

Questions lead to Notes.

Articles inspire Reflections.

Observations become Ideas.

Ideas evolve into Projects.

Reflections become Evolutios.

Hermes is designed so that knowledge does not remain isolated.

Instead, every Item contributes to a continuously evolving understanding.
