# Questions

## Purpose

Questions are the foundation of deliberate learning inside Hermes.

Most learning applications encourage passive consumption. You read articles, watch videos, or highlight text and eventually move on.

Hermes takes a different approach.

Instead of beginning with answers, Hermes begins with questions.

A well-constructed question creates curiosity. It exposes gaps in your understanding and encourages you to think before seeking a solution.

Questions transform learning from passive reading into active reasoning.

---

## Why Questions Exist

Reading an explanation often creates the illusion of understanding.

Answering a question reveals whether that understanding actually exists.

Hermes intentionally presents Questions before explanations because the process of thinking is where learning truly happens.

Rather than asking you to remember information, Hermes encourages you to construct it yourself.

---

## Creating Questions

Questions can be created manually whenever curiosity arises.

You can also import hundreds or even thousands of questions through Manual Collections using structured JSON files.

This allows Hermes to gradually schedule meaningful questions over days, weeks, or months instead of overwhelming you all at once.

Questions may come from:

- Your own curiosity
- AI-generated question sets
- Books
- Courses
- Interview preparation
- Competitive programming
- Mathematics practice
- Research topics

Hermes treats every question equally.

---

## Today's Pursuit

Questions imported into Manual Collections do not immediately appear.

Instead, Hermes schedules them into Today's Pursuit according to the Daily Limit configured for that collection.

For example:

Daily Limit: 2 Questions

Today:

Question 1

Question 2

Tomorrow:

Question 3

Question 4

This creates a sustainable learning rhythm rather than an endless backlog.

---

## Solving a Question

Each Question follows the same deliberate workflow.

Question

↓

Think independently

↓

Write your answer

↓

Reveal the official answer

↓

Reflect (Optional)

↓

Complete

Every stage has a purpose.

Hermes intentionally delays showing the official answer until after you have genuinely attempted the problem.

---

## Your Answer

Your answer represents your current understanding.

It is never compared for correctness automatically.

Instead, Hermes preserves it as a snapshot of how you thought at that moment.

Over time, this becomes an incredibly valuable record of your learning journey.

Even after completion, your answer remains editable.

Understanding evolves, and your explanations should be free to evolve as well.

---

## Official Solution

Once you reveal the solution, Hermes presents the original answer alongside your own.

The goal is not to score yourself.

The goal is to compare reasoning.

Sometimes your answer may be different yet equally valid.

Sometimes a single missing concept becomes obvious immediately.

Learning happens through comparison rather than memorization.

---

## Reflection

Reflection is optional.

Not every question changes your thinking.

However, when it does, Hermes encourages you to record what changed.

Examples include:

"I misunderstood time complexity."

"I finally understood why recursion works."

"I kept confusing processes with threads."

Reflections capture understanding rather than correctness.

Like your answer, reflections remain editable even after completion.

---

## Completing a Question

Completing a question records your progress.

Hermes preserves:

- Your answer
- The official solution
- Your reflection
- Completion date

Completed questions remain available for future review.

Learning should never disappear after pressing a button.

---

## Reviewing Completed Questions

At any time, you can reopen a completed question.

Hermes displays:

- The original question
- Your answer
- The official solution
- Your reflection
- Completion date

This allows you to revisit your earlier thinking and observe how your understanding has evolved.

---

## Skip for Today

Sometimes today simply is not the right day.

Instead of forcing yourself through every question, Hermes allows you to skip it.

Skipping:

- Removes the question from Today's Pursuit
- Immediately schedules another eligible question
- Does not mark the question as completed
- Allows it to return on a future day

This keeps your momentum without losing valuable questions.

---

## Why This Workflow?

Most learning software measures completion.

Hermes measures engagement.

A completed checkbox says very little.

A written answer, thoughtful comparison, and personal reflection reveal genuine understanding.

Questions exist to challenge your thinking, not simply to test your memory.

Every completed question becomes another step toward deeper understanding rather than another item crossed off a list.
