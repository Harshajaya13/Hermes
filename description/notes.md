# Notes

## Purpose

Notes preserve understanding in your own words.

Most note-taking applications become digital storage rooms filled with copied text, screenshots, and links. Months later, it becomes difficult to remember which notes mattered and which were simply copied for "later."

Hermes encourages a different philosophy.

A Note should represent what *you understand*, not what someone else wrote.

Instead of becoming another archive of information, Notes become an archive of your thinking.

---

## Why Notes Exist

Understanding becomes stronger when ideas are rewritten in your own words.

After reading an article, solving a problem, or learning a new concept, there is often a moment where everything suddenly becomes clear.

That understanding deserves its own place.

Notes capture those moments before they disappear.

Rather than asking:

> "What did this article say?"

Hermes encourages:

> "How would I explain this to someone else?"

That simple shift transforms information into knowledge.

---

## Writing Notes

Notes use plain Markdown.

Markdown was intentionally chosen because it is simple, readable, portable, and future-proof.

Your knowledge should never depend on proprietary file formats or cloud services.

Years from now, your Notes should remain just as readable as the day you wrote them.

Hermes stores your knowledge in a format that belongs to you.

---

## Connecting Notes

Knowledge rarely exists in isolation.

While writing a Note, you can connect it to other Items across your workspace.

For example:

Operating Systems

↓

Process Scheduling

↓

Connected Note

↓

CPU Context Switching

These connections create a growing network of understanding instead of isolated documents.

As your workspace expands, related ideas naturally become easier to discover.

---

## Keywords

Each Note can include custom keywords.

Keywords improve discoverability without changing the structure of your workspace.

For example, a Note about SQLite might include:

- Database
- SQL
- B-Tree
- Storage Engine
- Indexing

Later, searching any of these terms immediately surfaces the Note.

Keywords complement your organization rather than replacing it.

---

## Editing Notes

Understanding changes over time.

Hermes encourages continuous refinement rather than permanent drafts.

Every Note remains editable.

As you gain new insights, you can revisit previous Notes, clarify explanations, expand ideas, and improve your understanding without creating unnecessary duplicates.

A Note grows alongside you.

---

## Notes as Building Blocks

Many larger ideas begin as small Notes.

A single Note may eventually inspire:

- An Observation
- A Reflection
- An Idea
- A Project
- An Evolutio

Nothing inside Hermes exists in isolation.

Knowledge naturally grows through connections.

---

## Long-Term Value

Over months and years, your Notes become more than documentation.

They become a personal textbook written entirely by you.

Instead of searching the internet for the same explanations repeatedly, you gradually build a collection of concepts explained in the way that makes the most sense to you.

No one understands your learning style better than your past self.

---

## Why This Workflow?

Copying information is effortless.

Explaining it in your own words requires understanding.

Hermes values the second far more than the first.

Notes exist to preserve your understanding, allowing your future self to revisit not just what you learned, but how you understood it.
