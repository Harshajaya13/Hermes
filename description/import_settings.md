# Import Settings

## Purpose

Importing knowledge is only the first step.

Import Settings determine how that knowledge behaves once it enters Hermes.

Rather than treating every imported collection the same, Hermes allows you to configure where the collection belongs, how often it appears, and how it integrates into your daily workflow.

These settings ensure that every collection follows your own learning pace.

---

## Why Import Settings Exist

Imagine importing:

- 500 Probability Questions
- 800 Linux Articles
- 300 PyTorch Exercises
- 200 Research Papers

If Hermes simply displayed everything immediately, your workspace would become overwhelming.

Instead, every collection carries its own behavior.

Import Settings define that behavior before the collection is saved.

---

## Domain Selection

Every imported collection belongs to a Domain.

Examples include:

- Engineering
- Mathematics
- Startup
- Philosophy
- Health

Domains provide the highest level of organization inside Hermes.

Selecting the correct Domain ensures that related knowledge naturally stays together.

---

## Block Selection

Within every Domain, knowledge belongs to a Block.

For example:

Engineering

↓

Python

or

Engineering

↓

Operating Systems

or

Mathematics

↓

Probability

Blocks keep subjects focused while allowing Domains to remain broad and uncluttered.

---

## Collection Type

Hermes currently supports multiple collection types.

Examples include:

- Questions
- Articles

Each type follows its own workflow after import.

Questions become part of active learning.

Articles become part of intentional reading.

Future versions of Hermes may introduce additional collection types while preserving the same philosophy.

---

## Daily Limit

One of the most important settings is the Daily Limit.

This controls how many items Hermes schedules from a collection each day.

For example:

Probability Questions

Daily Limit

2

Hermes will only schedule two unanswered questions each day.

Tomorrow, another two appear.

This keeps learning consistent instead of overwhelming.

Every collection can have its own independent Daily Limit.

---

## Scheduling

Importing a collection does **not** immediately add every item to Today's Pursuit.

Instead, Hermes stores the collection locally and waits for the scheduler.

Each day, the scheduler selects eligible items according to the collection's Daily Limit.

This creates a continuous learning experience rather than a massive backlog.

---

## Editing Settings

Import Settings are never permanent.

As your learning changes, you can revisit a collection and update:

- Domain
- Block
- Daily Limit
- Collection information

Hermes grows with you.

Your organization should remain flexible rather than rigid.

---

## Local Ownership

All settings are stored locally alongside the collection.

Nothing depends on cloud synchronization or external services.

Your scheduling preferences remain entirely under your control.

---

## Philosophy

Knowledge should not only be organized.

It should arrive at the right pace.

Import Settings exist to ensure that learning remains intentional, sustainable, and adapted to the way you work rather than forcing you into someone else's system.
