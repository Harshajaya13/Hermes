# Manual Collections

## Purpose

Knowledge should never enter your mind by accident.

Modern applications constantly push information toward you.

Recommended videos.

Suggested articles.

Trending posts.

Infinite feeds.

Hermes intentionally moves in the opposite direction.

Instead of pushing knowledge toward you, Hermes allows you to deliberately bring knowledge into your own system.

Manual Collections are the beginning of that process.

They are the place where your long-term knowledge journey starts.

---

## Why Manual Collections Exist

Imagine finding one hundred excellent articles about Deep Learning.

Reading all of them in a single week is unrealistic.

Even if you tried, very little would be retained.

Instead of treating knowledge as something to consume immediately, Hermes allows you to build a personal knowledge pipeline.

You collect knowledge once.

Hermes gradually introduces it into your daily learning through Today's Pursuit.

This transforms overwhelming collections into sustainable daily progress.

---

## What Is a Manual Collection?

A Manual Collection is a local container that stores knowledge you intentionally imported.

Each collection belongs to a specific Domain and Block.

For example:

Engineering

↓

Deep Learning

↓

Manual Collection

Inside a collection you may store:

- Questions
- Articles

Each collection remains completely local and fully under your control.

---

## Importing Knowledge

Hermes currently supports importing structured JSON files.

Rather than manually creating hundreds of Questions or Articles one by one, you can prepare them once and import them in seconds.

This makes it practical to build large knowledge collections that continue serving you for months.

For example:

• 500 Probability Questions

• 1,000 Linux Articles

• 300 PyTorch Exercises

• Research Papers

Everything becomes part of your personal learning system.

---

## Daily Scheduling

Importing knowledge does not immediately flood your workspace.

Instead, every collection has a configurable Daily Limit.

For example:

Probability Questions

Daily Limit:

2 Questions

Every day Hermes selects two unanswered questions from the collection and places them into Today's Pursuit.

Tomorrow another two appear.

The collection slowly unfolds over time instead of becoming an overwhelming backlog.

---

## Full Ownership

Manual Collections belong entirely to you.

Nothing depends on an external server.

Nothing disappears because a website changes.

Everything is stored inside your own workspace.

Your knowledge remains portable, searchable, and completely under your control.

---

## Collection Settings

Every Manual Collection provides its own configuration.

You can decide:

• Which Domain it belongs to

• Which Block it belongs to

• Daily scheduling limits

• Item type

This allows every collection to behave independently.

For example:

Algorithms

3 Questions per day

Linux

1 Article per day

Probability

5 Questions per day

Every collection follows its own rhythm.

---

## The Knowledge Pipeline

Manual Collections are the beginning of Hermes' knowledge pipeline.

Collect once.

Import once.

Learn gradually.

Instead of asking yourself every morning:

"What should I study today?"

Hermes quietly answers that question for you.

The system transforms your long-term curiosity into consistent daily learning without requiring constant planning.

---

## Why This Workflow?

Most applications help you collect information.

Very few help you consume it intentionally.

Manual Collections exist because learning should be deliberate, sustainable, and completely owned by you.

Rather than chasing today's recommendations, you build your own library and allow Hermes to guide you through it one thoughtful step at a time.

Knowledge enters because you invited it.

Never because an algorithm decided for you.
