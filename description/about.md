# About Hermes

## Why Hermes Exists

Hermes was never created to become another productivity application.

It was created to solve a problem that became increasingly obvious over time.

Information had become effortless to collect.

Understanding had become increasingly difficult to preserve.

Articles were saved and forgotten.

Bookmarks accumulated faster than they were read.

Notes became scattered across multiple applications.

Ideas disappeared because they were never revisited.

Questions were answered once and never seen again.

The problem was never access to information.

The problem was building a system that could slowly transform information into understanding.

Hermes exists to solve that problem.

---

## A Different Philosophy

Most applications focus on helping you collect more.

Hermes focuses on helping you understand more.

Instead of endless feeds, Hermes gives you Today's Pursuit.

Instead of streaks, Hermes gives you Veritas.

Instead of counting completed tasks, Hermes preserves Evolutios.

Instead of storing everything as notes, Hermes recognizes that Questions, Articles, Ideas, Observations, Reflections, and Notes all represent different stages of learning.

Every feature inside Hermes exists because it supports understanding.

Nothing exists simply because it looks productive.

---

## Local First

Your knowledge belongs to you.

Hermes was designed around a simple principle:

If you spent years building your understanding, you should never lose ownership of it.

Your Workspaces live on your device.

Your Notes remain yours.

Your Questions remain yours.

Your Ideas remain yours.

No subscriptions are required to access your own thinking.

No cloud service is required to preserve it.

Hermes serves your knowledge.

Your knowledge should never serve Hermes.

---

## Learn Slowly

Modern software encourages constant consumption.

More videos.

More articles.

More notifications.

More recommendations.

Hermes intentionally slows everything down.

Rather than overwhelming you with hundreds of items, Hermes gradually surfaces carefully scheduled knowledge through Today's Pursuit.

Learning should feel sustainable.

Not exhausting.

Small progress repeated consistently becomes extraordinary over time.

---

## Build Your Own Library

Hermes does not decide what you should learn.

You do.

Import your own Questions.

Save your own Articles.

Write your own Notes.

Record your own Observations.

Develop your own Ideas.

Reflect in your own words.

Hermes simply provides the environment where that knowledge can continue growing for years.

Your library reflects your curiosity, not someone else's algorithm.

---

## Growth Over Productivity

Finishing work is important.

Changing the way you think is even more important.

Hermes measures growth through understanding rather than activity.

A single Evolutio may matter more than dozens of completed tasks.

A thoughtful Reflection may outlast an entire week's productivity.

The goal has never been to do more.

The goal has always been to understand more deeply.

---

## Built To Last

Hermes was designed with a long-term perspective.

Knowledge collected today should remain useful years from now.

Ideas should be revisited.

Questions should continue teaching.

Articles should remain searchable.

Reflections should reveal your growth.

The application should become more valuable with time, not less.

Every year you spend using Hermes should make the next year even richer.

---

## An Invitation

Hermes is not a finished destination.

It is a companion for continuous learning.

Whether you're studying engineering, building a startup, researching new ideas, or simply trying to understand the world a little better, Hermes grows alongside you.

The knowledge inside Hermes is not a database.

It is a reflection of your curiosity.

And over time, it becomes a reflection of who you are becoming.

---

## Final Thought

Information is easy to collect.

Understanding is difficult to preserve.

Hermes exists to bridge the distance between the two.
