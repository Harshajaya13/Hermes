# Ideas

## Purpose

Ideas are possibilities waiting to grow.

Not every thought arrives fully formed. Some begin as a single sentence, a moment of curiosity, or a problem that refuses to leave your mind.

Hermes treats Ideas as living pieces of knowledge rather than temporary notes.

Instead of expecting an idea to be complete on the first day, Hermes provides an environment where it can mature naturally over time.

---

## Why Ideas Exist

Many good ideas disappear because they are captured too late.

Others disappear because they are written down once and never revisited.

Hermes was designed to solve both problems.

An Idea is not expected to be perfect.

It only needs to exist.

Once recorded, it can gradually evolve through expansion, experimentation, connections, and reflection.

Great projects rarely begin as detailed plans.

Most begin as incomplete thoughts.

---

## Recording an Idea

Creating an Idea should be effortless.

Give it a short title that captures the essence of the thought.

Examples:

- Local AI Knowledge System
- Better FFmpeg Timeline
- Mathematical Visualizer
- Linux Workflow Improvements

The title is only the beginning.

Everything else can evolve later.

---

## Expand Idea

Ideas rarely stop growing after their first sentence.

The **Expand Idea** section allows you to continuously develop the original thought.

Add:

- new discoveries
- design decisions
- technical approaches
- challenges
- future improvements

Unlike rewriting the title, expansion preserves the original idea while documenting how it grows over time.

Months later, you'll be able to see exactly how a small thought evolved into something much larger.

---

## Potential Applications

Every idea has multiple possible directions.

Instead of mixing them into the main description, Hermes provides a dedicated section for Potential Applications.

Here you can explore:

- where the idea could be useful
- who might benefit from it
- possible products
- research opportunities
- startup possibilities

Separating applications from the core idea keeps both sections focused while encouraging broader thinking.

---

## Connect Related Items

Ideas rarely exist in isolation.

An Idea can be connected with other knowledge inside Hermes.

For example:

Idea

↓

Related Observation

↓

Supporting Article

↓

Relevant Note

↓

Solved Question

↓

Reflection

These connections allow ideas to grow using knowledge you've already collected.

Instead of searching through dozens of notes, everything related to an idea remains only a few clicks away.

Knowledge becomes a connected network rather than isolated documents.

---

## Promote to Project

Some ideas eventually become real work.

When an Idea reaches that stage, it can be promoted into a Project.

Promoted Ideas receive special visual emphasis, making them easy to identify among unfinished thoughts.

Promotion does not remove the idea.

It simply acknowledges that it has moved from possibility into execution.

If priorities change later, the Project status can be removed without losing any information.

---

## Archive

Not every idea needs immediate attention.

Some ideas are simply waiting for the right moment.

Archiving an Idea removes it from your active workspace while preserving everything attached to it.

Nothing is deleted.

Whenever the time feels right, the Idea can be restored exactly as it was.

Ideas deserve patience just as much as action.

---

## Revisiting Ideas

One of the most valuable habits inside Hermes is returning to old ideas.

A concept that seemed impossible six months ago may suddenly become achievable because your knowledge has grown.

Hermes encourages revisiting unfinished thoughts rather than constantly chasing new ones.

Progress often comes from developing existing ideas rather than replacing them.

---

## Long-Term Value

Over time, your Ideas become a record of your curiosity.

Some remain simple thoughts.

Some become personal experiments.

Some become research.

Some become startups.

Some quietly inspire completely different projects years later.

Hermes exists to preserve every stage of that journey.

---

## Why This Workflow?

Ideas are fragile.

Most disappear because they were never given room to grow.

Hermes provides that room.

By allowing ideas to expand, connect, evolve, and eventually become projects, Hermes transforms fleeting inspiration into something that can genuinely shape your future.

The goal is not to collect ideas.

The goal is to cultivate them.
