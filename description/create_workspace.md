# Create Workspace

## Purpose

A Workspace is the highest level of separation inside Hermes.

Instead of storing every piece of knowledge in one enormous database, Hermes allows you to create completely independent environments for different parts of your life.

Each Workspace contains its own Domains, Blocks, Items, Manual Collections, settings, and learning history.

Nothing is shared unless you intentionally export and import it.

---

## Why Workspaces Exist

Life is rarely made up of a single goal.

You might simultaneously be:

- Studying Engineering
- Building a Startup
- Preparing for GATE
- Learning Linux
- Writing personal journals
- Reading Philosophy

Combining all of these into one workspace quickly becomes overwhelming.

Hermes encourages separation instead of clutter.

Each Workspace becomes a focused environment dedicated to one purpose.

---

## Independent Knowledge Systems

Every Workspace is completely independent.

For example:

Engineering Workspace

↓

Questions

↓

Articles

↓

Notes

↓

Ideas

↓

Reflections

---

Startup Workspace

↓

Business Ideas

↓

Market Research

↓

Observations

↓

Projects

These two Workspaces never interfere with one another.

This keeps every environment clean and intentional.

---

## Creating a Workspace

Creating a Workspace takes only a moment.

Simply choose a name that represents its purpose.

Examples include:

- Engineering
- GATE Preparation
- Startup
- Personal Journal
- Research
- Mathematics

Once created, Hermes prepares a completely separate knowledge system for that Workspace.

You are free to organize it however you like.

---

## Starter Workspace

Every new installation of Hermes begins with a Starter Workspace.

The Starter Workspace exists purely as a learning environment.

Inside it, you'll discover examples of every supported Item type together with their workflows and settings.

It is designed to help you understand Hermes before building your own knowledge system.

Once you're comfortable, simply create your own Workspace and remove the Starter Workspace.

---

## Why Separation Matters

Imagine opening Hermes to study Probability.

Instead, you're greeted by:

- Startup ideas
- Linux notes
- Personal journals
- Travel observations

Your attention immediately fragments.

Workspaces solve this problem by keeping unrelated knowledge completely separate.

Each Workspace becomes a dedicated mental environment.

---

## Building From Scratch

A new Workspace begins empty.

This is intentional.

Rather than forcing a predefined structure, Hermes allows you to design your own Domains, Blocks, and knowledge pipeline according to your personal workflow.

Every Workspace reflects the way you think.

Not the way the software expects you to think.

---

## Local Ownership

Every Workspace is stored locally.

You control:

- Its structure
- Its contents
- Its backups
- Its exports

Nothing depends on cloud synchronization.

Nothing belongs to anyone except you.

---

## Philosophy

Your knowledge deserves context.

A Startup is not Engineering.

A Journal is not Mathematics.

A Research Library is not Daily Notes.

Hermes uses Workspaces to preserve those boundaries.

By separating different areas of your life, every Workspace remains calm, focused, and intentionally designed for the work you are doing today.
